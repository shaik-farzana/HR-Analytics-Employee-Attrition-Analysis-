# HR Analytics-Employee Attrition Analysis 

 ## 🔄 Project Overview

This project performs an end-to-end data analytics workflow on HR employee data to understand
the key drivers of employee attrition. 

Using Python for data preparation and EDA

SQL for business intelligence queries 

Power BI for interactive dashboards

This project simulates a corporate-grade analytics pipeline that translates raw HR data into actionable business

> **Core Business Problem:** Why are employees leaving the company — and how can we prevent it?

Step 3 — Load Data into MySQL

Step 5 — Dashboard (Power BI)


## 📊 Dataset Overview

Dataset	HR Analytics — Employee Attrition

Total Records	1,480 employees

Total Features	38 columns

Target Variable	Attrition (Yes / No)

Attrition — Yes	238 employees (16.08%)

Attrition — No	1,242 employees (83.92%)


## 📊 Key Findings
📈 Attrition Rate: 16.2% overall — above the healthy industry benchmark of 10%

💰 Salary Impact: Employees in "Upto 5k" salary slab show significantly higher attrition

🏢 Department Risk: Sales department has the highest attrition rate among all departments

⏰ Overtime Effect: Employees working overtime are far more likely to leave the company

👥 Age Pattern: Younger employees in the 18–25 age group have the highest attrition rate

😞 Satisfaction: Employees who left had consistently lower job satisfaction scores

🔄 Tenure Risk: Employees with 0–2 years at company are at highest risk of leaving

💼 Role Risk: Sales Representatives and Laboratory Technicians have highest turnover


## 💡 Business Recommendations

Revise salary structure for "Upto 5k" slab employees

Implement overtime policy and monitor workload,we can improve retention by 15%

Launch onboarding retention program for new joiners (0–2 yrs)

Create clear career growth paths for Sales Representatives

Introduce mentorship program for employees aged 18–25
